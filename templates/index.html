Solar panels as the first step

For many homes, solar is the most attainable place to start. It is often more affordable than adding batteries right away, and it can deliver a strong long-term return by lowering electricity bills over time.

That is why we are leading with solar in this pilot. When neighbours move together, installers can often reduce soft costs like sales, travel, and scheduling overhead. That can translate into better group pricing, clearer options, and a simpler process for everyone.

Battery backup instead of a gas generator

Many households are considering generators because of storms and power outages. A battery backup system can provide quiet, automatic emergency power without gasoline storage, engine maintenance, fumes, or noise.

Batteries are not the right answer for every home. They can be expensive. But if several households are already considering generators, a coordinated solar-plus-battery option may be worth comparing clearly and honestly.

Could roof insulation be added at the same time as solar?

Adding exterior insulation can be a great option for flat roofs, or for homes that are already planning a full re-roofing project. In those cases, coordinating work can sometimes improve comfort and efficiency while crews are already mobilized.

For most pitched-roof homes, we will also ask contractors and advisors about interior attic insulation and air sealing options, so residents can compare practical paths for their specific home.

How neighbours build momentum

People act faster when they see nearby homes moving first. This process is designed to make the choice visible, social, and easy to join.

  1. Register interest. Share your name, email, and general location. No purchase commitment.
  2. Watch the count grow. The cohort tracker shows how close the neighbourhood is to the next discount threshold.
  3. Invite local contractors. Once the group is large enough, we ask qualified contractors to quote for the cluster of homes.
  4. Compare options openly. Residents see equipment, warranties, timelines, and pricing side by side.
  5. Choose individually. Every household decides on its own. Group momentum makes the process easier, not mandatory.
  6. Share what worked. Yard signs, window decals, and short neighbour stories help the next block move faster.

If your home already performs well, you still matter. Climate Pioneers make efficient choices visible, answer questions, and set the local norm.

Open-source outreach kit

The Invisible-to-Visible Toolkit

Solar is easy to spot. Heat pumps, insulation, air sealing, and batteries are not. These open templates help neighbours make the invisible work visible on the street.

Print-ready yard signs and window decals

Use neighbourhood signs and window stickers to show that a home is part of the cohort. Example copy: “This home is upgrading to a heat pump with the {{ location.city }} Cohort. Ask me how.”

Suggested repo paths: /assets/signs/yard-sign.svg, /assets/signs/window-decal.svg, /assets/signs/print/.

Micro-local flyer templates

Use short flyers for each block, lane, or street. Example copy: “Did you know 12 homes on our block are currently pricing out solar panels?” That kind of local proof is often stronger than a savings estimate alone.

Suggested repo paths: /assets/flyers/block-flyer.md, /assets/flyers/block-flyer.pdf, /templates/outreach.html.
