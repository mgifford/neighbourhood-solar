For solar and home energy contractors

Stop chasing scattered one-off jobs. Build a neighbourhood pipeline.

Most residential opportunities arrive one household at a time, across a wide geography, with high sales effort before scope is even clear. This pilot organizes interested homeowners in the same area into one transparent cohort, so contractors can quote and deliver more efficiently.

The business case

Volts and other industry voices have highlighted a core reality in residential solar: customer acquisition and sales overhead are often among the biggest cost pressures. This pilot addresses that problem directly by grouping motivated homeowners before contractors begin full proposal work.

Instead of running separate sales cycles for scattered leads, you engage a pre-qualified local cohort with shared timelines, shared questions, and clearer demand signals.

Operational benefits

  • Pre-qualified homeowners concentrated in one area
  • Batch site assessments instead of isolated truck rolls
  • Coordinated scheduling across neighbouring properties
  • Lower travel, mobilization, and sales overhead per project
  • Faster conversion through group information sessions

Strategic upside

  • More predictable pipeline quality and install planning
  • Higher trust from transparent, open comparison
  • Stronger referrals from visible neighbourhood results
  • A platform for premium whole-home service offerings
  • Early positioning for future neighbourhood cohorts

The upsell opportunity: what homeowners actually want

Residents are not asking for a single product. They are asking for integrated outcomes: lower bills, resilient backup power, better comfort, and a process they can trust.

The winning offer is not solar only. It is a coordinated scope led by contractors who can deliver solar, batteries, and electrification with clear project management.

Core demand in this pilot

  • Solar panels
  • Battery backup as an alternative to gas generators
  • Heat pumps for heating and cooling
  • EV charging integrated with home energy upgrades
  • Guidance on incentive and financing pathways

Roof and insulation bundling opportunity

  • Residents want to coordinate solar timing with planned roof replacement projects
  • Exterior insulation is a priority for suitable flat-roof projects
  • For pitched roofs, residents are asking about attic insulation and air sealing
  • Many households prefer one contractor-led scope instead of fragmented trades
  • Contractors who can coordinate this end-to-end will stand out

How the pilot works

This is an open process. No contractor is pre-selected, and no backroom agreements are made. It is also not a race to the bottom on price: residents are explicitly looking for premium equipment options, reliable {{ cohort.opening_ask.workmanship_warranty_years }}-year workmanship coverage, and high-quality execution they can trust long term.

  1. Express interest. Let us know you want to participate. No commitment at this stage.
  2. Review the cohort profile. Once enough nearby homes register, we share aggregate demand signals and likely scope ranges.
  3. Submit a transparent value proposal. Include equipment tiers, clear pricing structure, warranty terms, and group thresholds for {% for t in cohort.thresholds %}{{ t }}{% if not loop.last %}, {% endif %}{% endfor %} homes.
  4. Join a group information session. Address resident questions once, openly, with peers present.
  5. Contract household by household. Each homeowner chooses independently and signs directly with the selected contractor.
  6. Publish aggregate outcomes. Non-sensitive results are shared to improve future cohorts and market trust.

The long game

Ottawa's direction is clear: more building decarbonization, more electrification, and higher expectations for resilient, efficient homes. Contractors that build repeatable, whole-home delivery models now will be better positioned for where the market is heading.

This pilot is designed as a practical collaboration between residents and contractors to build that model in the open.
