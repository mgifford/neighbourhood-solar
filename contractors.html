Neighbourhood Solar
Residents Contractors Community Leaders

For solar and home energy contractors

A pilot to reduce acquisition costs and deploy neighbourhood-scale solar faster.

We are exploring a transparent community procurement model that brings together qualified homeowners in a concentrated geographic area.

The business case

Residential solar is often slowed by customer acquisition, education, scheduling, and site-by-site sales work. A neighbourhood cohort can reduce friction before a contractor spends time producing detailed proposals.

The intent is not to demand an unrealistic discount. The intent is to identify real operational savings and share part of that value with homeowners.

Potential value for contractors

  • Pre-qualified local leads
  • Coordinated information sessions
  • Batch site assessments
  • More efficient scheduling
  • Reduced travel and mobilization
  • Potential procurement efficiencies
  • Community visibility and referrals

What residents may request

  • Transparent price per watt
  • Group pricing thresholds for 5, 10, and 20 homes
  • Premium panel and inverter options
  • Battery backup package options
  • Longer workmanship warranty
  • Clear production assumptions
  • Plain-language financing support

Proposed opening ask

For a qualified neighbourhood cohort, we would like contractors to consider:

  • Upgraded equipment option, such as premium panels and high-quality inverter architecture
  • 10% group discount as an opening position
  • 10-year workmanship warranty
  • Battery backup options designed around critical loads
  • Publicly documented aggregate results after installation

The expected compromise may be closer to upgraded equipment, a 5% reduction, and an extended workmanship warranty. Contractors are invited to explain what is operationally realistic.
